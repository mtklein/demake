# Under Selûne — liner notes

*Jukebox track 11. A love ballad in the "Eyes on Me" idiom, named for Selûne,
the moon goddess Shadowheart once served. Written for the survivors on the
Ravaged Beach: two people who woke with worms behind their eyes and a world on
fire, and got one quiet night under the moon before whatever comes next.*

> In the jukebox (SELECT on the title screen), pressing A on *Under Selune* opens a
> synced karaoke view: the current line in gold, the next in grey, following the
> music row by row.

The melody was composed first, as an instrumental line — channel 0 is a
square-wave "voice." These words were written afterward to fit that line
**note for note**: one syllable per attack, the open vowels placed on the
longest-held notes so they land where a singer would breathe and lean. The
verse sits low and close; the chorus jumps an octave; the final chorus lifts
the whole thing another whole step into D major — the genre's sacred gear-change,
placed so the highest note in the song falls on the word *you*.

---

## Verse 1

> I found you by the tide, and slept
> The far sky burned away, smoke rose
> You breathed, you were alive — with me
> and all that's left is you —

## Pre-chorus

> if the morning takes my name,
> hold on to me now, please —

## Chorus

> Hold us close, under dawn —
> Selûne, you know our names;
> you stayed when all was gone,
> you stayed with me.

## Verse 2

> I never said the things I meant to say,
> the fear behind my eyes, I kept from you —
> but salt and sand, and you asleep at last,
> there's nothing left to hide —

*(pre-chorus and chorus repeat)*

## Final chorus — up a whole step, into D

> so shine — Selûne, you know our names,
> what we were remains,
> when all else goes — you stay with me.

## How the words sit on the notes

The chorus hook, showing the fit (note · held-rows in parens, CAPS = the long
notes that carry the open vowels):

```
  HOLD  us   close  |  un  -der  DAWN   |  Se  -lûne  YOU
  C5(10) A4   C5(4)    B4(8) A4   E5(10)   D5   B4(4) C5(10)

  know  our  NAMES  |  you  STAYED   |  when all  was  GONE
  B4    A4   D5(8)    C5   A4(8)      G4(6) A4  B4   C5(12)

  you  STAYED  |  with  me
  D5   E5(8)     D5    C5(6)
```

`dawn`, `gone`, and both `stayed`s ride the biggest notes — `gone` gets the
12-row monster in bar 6, and in the final chorus the transposition drops `you`
onto the F#5 that's the highest pitch in the piece. The intro, the turn, and
the outro are instrumental: there the answer-hook guitar (channel 1) does the
singing.
